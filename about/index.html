Marja Harmon

I started performing at age eight — because I love storytelling, but also because I’m shy. Onstage, I can be anyone: a proud lioness, a southern debutante, a destitute villager, a lovestruck warrior. Offstage, I’m still shy ol’ Marja who savors quiet moments, like watching milk marble into iced coffee. My life is a balance of practice and play. I practice gratitude. I practice kindness towards myself and others (the former is usually the more difficult task). I play with my friends, quoting random comedies and discussing life/love. And I’m incredibly #lucky to be able to play for a living (currently on Broadway in Book of Mormon). I’ve learned that soft or small does not equal insignificant. I find inspiration everywhere and always, but especially in: Christmas lights / folklore / long walks / Autumn / yoga / travel / Friends / Gabriel Garcia Marquez / Music / Paris (The Golden age, Audrey Tautou) / TED Talks / Rilke / laughter / breezes / New York City.

Bek Melocik

Grey is my favorite color. Gloomy days, mountains, moral complexities — I just love everything grey. When I’m not taking deep breaths or discussing the beautiful immensities of life, I take long exploratory walks around New York City. And I laugh a lot, mostly at banana humor, puns and impressions of anything (I do an awesome ‘teapot’). I published my first poem when I was eight years old (about the color green, my other fav shade), and I’ve been writing poetry, songs and stories ever since. Right now, I’m writing a musical about pigeons, totally my new fav animal (they’re intelligent, loyal, and so beautiful -- check out the colors on those feathers). I possess the rare talent of “rubber face,” and enjoy honing my funny/ugly/scary face technique, to my parents’ eternal dismay. My favorite material possession is a huge disco ball that hangs in my living room and acts as the epicenter for epic pajama dance parties.

Lexa Freshwater Burton

I used to live in St. Louis, Washington D.C., Seattle, L.A., and London. Now I live in Greenpoint, Brooklyn. I used to be reaaal competitive and wanted to win all the time. Now I love seeing other people succeed even more. I used to feel guilty unless I was always busy. Now my favorite hobby is just existing. I used to be internet famous for my Harry Potter fan videos. Now if you Google me a bunch of Lexa bindings by Burton Snowboards show up. I used to be afraid other people wouldn’t like me if I let them see me at Full Weird. Now I love that my spirit animal is a tree and I’ll tell you all about it if I see you down the rabbit hole.
